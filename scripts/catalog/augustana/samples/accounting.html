Accounting Major

The Department of Business Administration offers a comprehensive accounting program.

MAJOR IN ACCOUNTING. 32 credits including:

Required Core Courses

  • ACCT-200 Principles of Accounting I (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-201 Principles of Accounting II (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-313 Intermediate Accounting I (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-321 Intermediate Accounting I (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-322 Intermediate Accounting II (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-401 Advanced Accounting (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-410 Auditing (4 Credits)
  • ACCT-420 Federal Income Tax (4 Credits)

Supporting Courses

  • BUSN-215 Business Statistics (4 Credits, Q)
  • ECON-210 Principles of Microeconomics (4 Credits)
  • ECON-220 Principles of Macroeconomics (4 Credits)
  • MATH-115 Applied Calculus (4 Credits, Q)

Course Descriptions

ACCT-200 Principles of Accounting I (4 Credits)
An introduction to financial accounting concepts and principles.

ACCT-313 Intermediate Accounting I (4 Credits)
Advanced study of financial accounting theory and practice. Prerequisite: ACCT-201.

ACCT-321 Intermediate Accounting I (4 Credits)
Comprehensive coverage of accounting for assets, liabilities, and equity. Prerequisites: ACCT-313 and ACCT-200.

ACCT-401 Advanced Accounting (4 Credits)
Study of partnership accounting, corporate combinations, and international accounting. Prerequisite: ACCT-322.

BUSN-215 Business Statistics (4 Credits, Q)
Statistical methods for business decision making.

ECON-210 Principles of Microeconomics (4 Credits)
Introduction to microeconomic theory and applications.

Learning Outcomes

Students will develop proficiency in financial reporting, auditing, and tax preparation while gaining a strong foundation in business principles.
